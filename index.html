Why?

The world's oceans are vast and complex, covering over 70% of the Earth's surface. However, our understanding of these crucial bodies of water is limited by the lack of data we have available. This data gap is a significant problem, as it prevents us from fully comprehending the changes and processes occurring in the ocean, which are critical for the health of the planet and human society. To address this issue, we are developing a basic and affordable sensor that will collect temperature data and its location. By building this sensor, we hope to contribute to a better understanding of the ocean and its vital role in our world. With more accurate and comprehensive data, we can make better decisions to protect and sustain the oceans for future generations.

What is a Citizen Scientist?

A citizen scientist is an ordinary person who participates in scientific research and data collection, often in collaboration with professional scientists or research institutions. Citizen science projects can cover a wide range of topics, from astronomy and ecology to medicine and psychology, and typically involve volunteers collecting data or observations in their local environment or from online sources.

How can i become a citizen scientist?

To become a citizen scientist, you can start by finding a project that interests you and fits your skills and availability. There are many citizen science platforms and websites where you can browse projects and register as a volunteer. However, we think that OceanScope and our objective to fill the ocean data gap is a great starting point. Some projects may require specific equipment or training, while others may only require a smartphone and internet connection. In our case simple sensor equipment would be sufficient.

Where should i start?

if you are interested in contributing to citizen science efforts related to ocean data collection, you may want to consider using the OceanScope sensor, which is designed to measure various temperature at different locations in the ocean.

What industry will use my data?

The potential users of your data as a citizen scientist collecting ocean data will depend on the specific project and type of data you collect. However, if you focus on collecting data related to marine biodiversity and ocean health, you may be able to contribute to efforts that benefit both the tourism industry and the conservation and management of marine ecosystems.

You are ready!

By following these steps, you can become a valuable contributor to citizen science and help fill the ocean data gap, while also learning about marine conservation and biodiversity. Good luck!
